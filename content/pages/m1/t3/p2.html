Read this workplace scenario that relates to the industry you work in. It will be referred to throughout this module as your case study. You’ll use it to reflect on situations, answer questions and help develop the skills you need to manage customer misbehaviour.

Customer Service – face to face: Arlo arrives at the centre to complete a transaction. He’s feeling flustered after missing the first bus to get there. When Arlo approaches the counter, the service worker, Simone, advises that he doesn’t have the required paperwork, so the transaction cannot be processed. Arlo becomes visibly agitated, pointing at Simone and raising his voice. He declares that he is not leaving the centre until the transaction has been processed.

Customer Service - phone: Fatima calls the customer service line regarding an application she submitted but hasn’t yet received a response for. Without allowing the service worker, James, to speak, Fatima demands an update, saying she has important things to do. James attempts to explain that the request is in progress, but that it takes seven days to process, and only five days have elapsed. Fatima asks to speak to the manager.

Finance: Alex is at the branch service desk. He has been advised by the service worker, Elliot, that his enquiry cannot be resolved today but will be resolved by close of business tomorrow. Alex rolls his eyes and begins speaking louder so other customers can hear. He makes comments, such as, ‘Typical - all these businesses are the same’ and, ‘Now you’re making me wait to get my own money!’

Fast Food: Owen has been waiting in line for his lunch order and he’s in a hurry to get back to the office. Ren is working at the front counter and has recently completed his trainee program. He can see Owen in the queue getting annoyed, folding his arms and tapping his foot. Owen is starting to irritate other customers in front of him, saying, ‘Can you believe how long this is taking?’ When Owen reaches the counter, he angrily hits the counter and says loudly, ‘Finally! Do you think you can get this done quickly?’

Healthcare: Health services have been inundated with patients during the last fortnight due to unavailability of medical staff. Rose approaches the reception counter, advising she is 30 minutes late for her appointment. Freya lets Rose know the next available time is now tomorrow at 5pm. Rose begins banging the counter and raising her voice, saying, ‘I need to see a doctor today! It’s not my fault the bus to get here was delayed!’ Rose is visibly upset and other people in the waiting room are noticeably uncomfortable.

Education: While helping Mira with an enquiry at the Student Enquiry Desk, Kai discovers her enrolment in the next course has not progressed due to insufficient grades in a pre-requisite subject. Kai begins to outline the reasons to Mira; however, Mira begins interrupting Kai, stating, ‘This is not acceptable. You have to fix this right now.’

Retail: Marcus approaches the sales counter and discovers there is a lengthy queue. He is in a hurry to get back to the office. Olga is on the sales counter and recently completed her trainee program. She can see Marcus is annoyed as he folds his arms and starts looking around, tapping his foot. Olga can see Marcus becoming irritated with other customers ahead of him. When he reaches the counter, he shoves his items towards her and leans over the counter, saying loudly, ‘This is absolutely ridiculous! I’ve got better things to do than wait around in your queue.’

Transport: As the bus driver, Luan, gets ready to depart, passenger Nadine sees another passenger board the bus without a face mask. Nadine asks the driver, ‘Why is this person allowed on the bus without a face mask?’ Nadine tries to bring other passengers into the dialogue, stating that it’s mandated for everyone to wear one. As Nadine taps off at her stop, she snarls at Luan, ‘Why didn’t you enforce the rules of wearing a face mask? This isn’t good enough. This impacts me!’
